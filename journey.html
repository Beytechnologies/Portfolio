The Hajj Journey

Hajj, the fifth pillar of Islam, is sacred and obligatory for every able adult Muslim. It is an extraordinary opportunity to embark on a profound spiritual expedition to the holiest city in Islam, Makkah. Just like the other important practices in Islam, Hajj comes with its own set of steps to be followed diligently during this sacred journey.

01 Ihram

Start your Hajj journey with a sincere intention to enter the state of Ihram and engage in the rituals of Hajj, with the recitation of the Talbiyah. Then, put on the Ihram garments.

02 Tawaf Al-Qudum

Upon entering Makkah, perform the Tawaf Al-Qudum by circumambulating the Kaaba seven times. Then, pray two Rak'ahs behind Maqam Ibrahim (if possible).

03 Sa'i between Safa and Marwa

Perform Sa'i between Safa and Marwah, walking seven times starting at Safa and ending at Marwah.

04 Mina (Day of Tarwiyah)

Head to Mina on the morning of the 8th of Dhul-Hijjah and spend the night in prayer until the morning of the 9th.

05 Arafah

On the 9th of Dhul-Hijjah, head to Arafat and devote yourself to Allah until sunset.

06 Muzdalifah

After sunset, move to Muzdalifah, spend the night there, and collect pebbles for Jamarat.

07 Pelt Jamrat Al-Aqabah

On the 10th of Dhul-Hijjah, pelt Jamrat Al-Aqabah with seven pebbles while reciting Takbeer.

08 Adhahi

Offer Adhahi (sacrifice) to draw closer to Allah and share with those in need.

09 Shave Hair or Trim

Shave or trim your hair. Then, remove the Ihram and change into your regular clothes.

10 Tawaf Al-Ifada

Return to Makkah and perform Tawaf Al-Ifada, circumambulating the Kaaba seven times and praying behind Maqam Ibrahim.

11 Pelting the three Jamarat

On the 11th, 12th, and optionally 13th of Dhul-Hijjah, pelt the three Jamarat in Mina.

12 Tawaf Al-Wadaa

Before leaving Makkah, perform the farewell Tawaf (Tawaf Al-Wadaa) by circumambulating the Kaaba seven times.
